Roof Color FAQ (Southeast)

What is the most popular shingle color in the Southeast?

Weathered Wood and Charcoal/Black are the two most installed families. Weathered Wood pairs with brick and warm siding; Charcoal/Black maximizes contrast and hides valleys well.

Is there a difference between “Pewter Gray” and “Pewter”?

Yes—naming varies by brand. GAF uses Pewter Gray; Atlas/IKO often use Pewter. Both are mid-grays; sample boards show subtle differences in warmth and speckle.

Weathered Wood vs. Driftwood—how do I choose?

Weathered Wood leans brown/earthy; Driftwood trends cooler gray. Match to gutter, brick, and fascia colors; view both in sun and shade.

Do “cool” shingles really reduce attic temps?

Cool-rated colors (e.g., Atlas Pinnacle Sun/Cool) reflect more solar energy. Expect a modest improvement; combined with proper ventilation, you’ll see the best results.

What are the common PVDF metal roof colors in our region?

Charcoal, Matte Black, Burnished Slate, Galvalume Plus, Hartford Green, Roman/Gallery Blue, and Saddle/Light Stone are widely stocked in AL/GA.

Color Comparisons People Ask For (Southeast)

Across Alabama, Georgia, Mississippi, and the Florida Panhandle, homeowners often search for color match-ups before they call a roofer. If you’re comparing any of the pairs below, you’re in the right place:

  • Charcoal vs Pewter (Gray family) – Charcoal is the darkest, Pewter/Pewter Gray is a softer mid-gray.
  • Weathered Wood vs Driftwood (Brown/Gray blend) – Weathered Wood skews browner; Driftwood has cooler gray notes.
  • Black Shadow / Dual Black vs Onyx/Moire Black – Brand names for “black” vary; undertones (warm/cool) differ slightly.
  • Hunter/Forest Green vs Hartford Green – Metal and shingle greens vary by sheen; always compare in sunlight and shade.
