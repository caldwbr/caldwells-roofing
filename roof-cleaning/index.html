  • Flat roofs (PVC/TPO/EPDM): pollen/dirt film, drain/scupper grime.

  • Concrete: driveways, sidewalks, porches, patios.

How We Clean Roofs (Safely)

Warning about degranulation: Pressure washing can dislodge some granules off your shingles. This tradeoff may be worth it to improve appearance.

Honest trade-off: Any roof wash can cause a small amount of degranulation (some granules will release). Many shingle warranties caution against pressure washing for this reason. We’ll discuss the condition of your roof up front, the cleaning method we recommend, and whether the aesthetic benefit outweighs potential warranty concerns in your specific case.

Metal & flat roofs: Typically respond well to low-pressure rinsing and appropriate cleaners, followed by a thorough fresh-water rinse. We avoid chemicals that could attack coatings or membranes.

When Cleaning Makes Sense

  • Your roof is otherwise sound (no major leaks or widespread shingle failure).
  • Black algae streaks are the main eyesore—common on north-facing slopes.
  • You’re preparing to sell and want fast curb appeal.
  • Ventilation and gutters are in good shape (we can also address those).

If your shingles are brittle, losing a lot of granules already, or the roof is near end-of-life, we’ll recommend repair or replacement options instead of cleaning.

House & Concrete Pressure Washing

  • House exterior: siding, soffit/fascia, gutter faces, porch ceilings. We use sensible pressure levels and fan tips to avoid paint or siding damage.
  • Driveways & sidewalks: circular head pressure washer and/or surface cleaners (if desired) to remove mildew, tire marks, and dirt evenly without zebra-striping.
