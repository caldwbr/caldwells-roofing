Cleaning and Stain Coating Cedar Shakes Roof

Proper maintenance of cedar shake roofs requires specialized knowledge and products to preserve the natural beauty and longevity of the wood. Our professional approach combines biological cleaning with premium penetrating oils to protect your investment.

We use D2 biological cleaner as our primary cleaning solution because it offers significant advantages over traditional pressure washing:

  • Biological Action: Uses enzymatic formulas to safely break down black algae, moss, and lichen without damaging the wood
  • No Pressure Washing Required: Gentle application preserves the wood's natural protective lignin coating
  • Preserves Lignin: Unlike harsh chemicals, D2 doesn't eat away at the wood's natural protective outer layer
  • Environmentally Friendly: Biodegradable formula is safe for surrounding landscaping

After thorough cleaning, we apply Penofin Brazilian Rosewood penetrating oil for long-lasting protection:

  • Deep Penetration: Rosewood oil carries UV-blocking pigments deep into wood fibers
  • Enhanced Longevity: 2-5 years protection on low-slope roofs, up to 5-10 years on steep-slope roofs
  • Natural Beauty: Enhances wood grain while providing superior weather protection

Understanding Cedar Aging and Maintenance

Cedar shakes naturally age and change appearance over time:

  • Initial State: Fresh cedar has warm, rich wood tones with visible grain patterns
  • Natural Aging: Over time, cedar develops a silver-grey patina from lignin exposure
  • Lignon Protection: This silver-grey coating is actually the wood's natural protective layer
  • Heavy Growth: If extensive algae or lichen has developed, gentle scrubbing with nylon brushes may be necessary, which can remove some of the protective lignin coat

Professional Roof Access and Safety

Many professional roofing contractors consider it acceptable to walk on cedar shake roofs when necessary, provided proper safety protocols are followed:

  • Full PPE Required: Fall protection harness systems are mandatory
  • Proper Footwear: Soft-soled shoes to minimize damage to shakes
  • Strategic Walking: Stepping on butt-ends of shakes where they're nailed, not in the middle
  • Minimal Impact: Careful movement patterns to preserve roof integrity
