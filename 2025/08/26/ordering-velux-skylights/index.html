Ordering VELUX Skylights

VELUX codes look cryptic, but once you know the pieces they’re simple. Below is the quick decode for mount type, venting, size codes (e.g., C06), and the finish/cladding/glass bundle (e.g., 2005). We also embedded the official VELUX size chart so you can cross-check dimensions.

TL;DR: FS = Fixed, deck-mounted. VS/VSE/VSS = Vented (manual/electric/solar), deck-mounted. CM/VCM/VFS = curb-mounted (build your own curb; best for low/flat roofs). C06 etc. = standard size codes. 2005 = interior 2 (white); cladding 0 (aluminum); glass 05 (Low-E3 tempered/laminated—varies by model).

Deck-Mounted vs Curb-Mounted

Deck-Mounted (FS/VS/VSE/VSS) fasten directly to the roof deck and use matched flashing kits with shingles or metal.

  • FS – Fixed (does not open)
  • VS – Vented (manual crank)
  • VSE – Vented (electric)
  • VSS – Vented (solar, remote)

Curb-Mounted (CM family: FCM/VCM/VCS/VFS) sit over a site-built curb. This is the go-to for flat or very low-slope roofs—you frame a curb (2x material), waterproof it (e.g., welded PVC/TPO with pre-formed corners), then set the skylight cap over it and fasten.

  • FCM – Fixed curb-mounted
  • VCM – Vented curb-mounted (manual)
  • VCE/VCS – Vented curb-mounted (electric/solar)

Decoding the Size (e.g., C06)

The letter maps to a width family; the number maps to a height. Common examples:

CodeTypical Rough Opening (approx.)Notes
Axx~14½" widthGreat for tight rafter spacing
Cxx~21" widthE.g., C06 very common
Mxx~30" widthLarger daylight opening
Sxx~44" widthBig, dramatic light

Flashing Kits (Don’t Skip These)

  • EDL – Shingle flashing kit (deck-mount)
  • EDM – Metal roof flashing (standing seam/AG panels; deck-mount)
  • ECL – Curb-mount flashing for shingles
  • Tile options – EDW/ECW, and multi-unit kits EKL/EKW

Flat/low-slope curb-mount installs typically don’t use a separate “kit”—the curb itself is flashed and waterproofed with your membrane (PVC/TPO/EPDM) and the skylight cap sheds over it.
