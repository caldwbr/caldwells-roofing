What Makes It “Superior”

  • System-specific checklists: We only inspect the items that apply to your roof type — no generic boxes. Coverage includes:
    • Steep-Slope: Asphalt shingles (3-tab, architectural, designer), metal (classic-rib, standing seam snap-lock/robot-seamed, metal shingles), copper (standing seam, flat-lock, specialty swoop/barrel), slate (real & synthetic), cedar shingles/shakes, Spanish/clay/concrete tile.
    • Low-Slope: PVC, TPO, EPDM, modified-bitumen, built-up (with/without gravel), copper flat-lock/sheet with soldering, and coatings (over SPF or metal).
  • Transitions & terminations: Valleys, step/counter-flashings, kick-outs, chimney saddles, shingle-to-membrane tie-ins, and perimeter metals are reviewed with photos.
  • Leak forensics: We look for slow-leak signatures (deck deflection, staining, silt lines), probe seams (where applicable), and trace likely water paths.
  • Ventilation sanity check: Intake vs. exhaust balance; conflicting systems; bath/kitchen vents terminating in attic.
  • Documentation pack: Photo set with notes/arrows, a Good/Watch/Fix summary, and a rough order-of-magnitude estimate (maintenance/repair/replacement).
  • Insurance-ready language: When storm damage is present, we document in a way adjusters understand.

Note: This is a non-invasive visual audit. Hidden conditions under roofing can require exploratory removal. If that’s advisable, we’ll state it clearly.
